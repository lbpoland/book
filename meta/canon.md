# CANON — TERMS & CONSTANTS

## Core Motifs (repeat and deepen)
- Image: representation through relationship, stewardship, and moral likeness
- Consent: influence requires invitation; coercion is forbidden
- Boundary: separation protects freedom without domination
- Trust: chosen loyalty under partial knowledge
- Memory: Watchers preserve truth when history distorts it
- Law: consequence woven into reality, not arbitrary punishment
- Time: mercy for growth; delay for repentance; arena for choice

## Named Core Figures (as used in this manuscript)
- Michael: restraint, defense, steadfast loyalty
- Gabriel: message, precision, timing
- Raphael: healing, restoration, sorrow-bearing mercy
- Uriel: illumination, insight, clarity
- The Watchers: memory, witness, non-intervention mandate
- The Bright One / Adversary: ideological drift; persuasion over force; patience

Canon Additions / Clarifications (Chapter 6)

Image defined as relational stewardship — Not physical resemblance but capacity for relationship, creativity, choice, and representation of God’s character.

Consent governs influence — No angel may influence beings of dust without invitation from God or the beings themselves; violation results in self-imposed isolation and loss of context.

Boundaries as gifts — Boundaries define spaces for freedom and protect purpose; they are not prisons but hospitable edges, physical and spiritual.

Law as consequence woven into reality — Actions align with outcomes; consequences teach and are not arbitrary punishments; angels are instructed not to remove natural consequences without invitation.

Time introduced as mercy — Time begins with the seen realm; it allows for change, growth, and repentance, and imposes patience; angels entering the seen realm must submit to its flow for the sake of those within.

Pedagogy through parable and patience — Illuminators are instructed to teach beings of dust through gradual revelation, parables, and by listening for consent; knowledge must be offered in stages.

Healing requires invitation and patience — Healers must wait for invitation before acting, even when pain is evident; presence and waiting are part of healing.

Mutual submission among orders — Guardians commit to confess and hold each other accountable to avoid overstepping boundaries; watchers witness these confessions.

Nuance in record-keeping — Watchers must capture tone and emphasis, not just words; records should reflect gentleness and firmness where both are present.

Beauty as clarity — Artistic representation (such as Lumiel’s tapestry) can clarify complex concepts; beauty is a tool for understanding.

Patience as healing posture — Waiting without immediate action is part of compassion and healing, especially in time-bound realms.

Messengers adopt humility — They must not outrun invitation; they translate messages in forms understandable and non-terrifying to beings of dust.

Pedagogy as empathy — Teaching must be done with sensitivity, using parables and listening for readiness; knowledge is covered until appropriate.

Practice as humility — Role‑playing scenarios helps angels internalize consent and boundaries; training fosters empathy and prepares hearts for service without control.

Seasons as teachers — Orifiel introduces the concept that seasons—spring, summer, autumn, winter—teach different lessons; angels should align messages and actions with seasonal rhythms and trust the cyclical nature of time.

Music as memory — Songs and ballads serve as living records; naming and motifs can be preserved through melody, turning memory into communal art.

Synergy across orders — Collaboration between different orders (healers and messengers, for example) enriches each gift; truth delivered with tenderness requires cooperation.

Seasons inform speech — Messages should be delivered in tones appropriate to the current season; the same truth may be spoken differently in spring than in winter.
